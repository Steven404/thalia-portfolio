# Product

## Register

brand

## Users

Adults and young adults seeking a private English teacher. They arrive from word-of-mouth or social discovery, often on mobile, in a casual context. They are deciding whether to trust Thalia with their learning journey — so credibility and warmth are what they are evaluating.

## Product Purpose

A personal portfolio for Thalia, a private English teacher offering lessons at all CEFR levels (A1–C2) in both English and Greek. The site exists to build trust, communicate professionalism, and capture contact leads. Success means a visitor reaching out.

## Brand Personality

Warm · Personal · Rigorous

## Anti-references

Corporate or cold learning platforms (HR training tools, enterprise e-learning). Should not feel institutional, mass-market, or sterile. Avoid anything that depersonalises the teacher–student relationship.

## Design Principles

1. **The teacher is the product.** Every design choice should foreground Thalia as a person, not a service.
2. **Trust before conversion.** Warmth and credibility come before CTAs. Don't rush the visitor.
3. **Bilingual by nature.** EN/EL is not a feature toggle — it is part of the identity. Both locales feel equally native.
4. **Restraint earns attention.** The site is small and focused. Every element earns its place.
5. **Craft signals quality.** Typography, spacing, and detail are the proof that lessons will be equally considered.

## Accessibility & Inclusion

WCAG AA as minimum. Body text contrast ≥ 4.5:1. Respect prefers-reduced-motion. Both EN and EL locales fully supported.
